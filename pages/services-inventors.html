Process

What happens next

The first step is your university's technology transfer office. From there, the path becomes clearer: the invention is assessed, protected where appropriate, and developed toward commercialization or, in some cases, a start-up route.

01

Report the invention

Before publishing your invention, contact the technology transfer office at your university. The invention is then forwarded to BayPAT so the next steps can be reviewed together.

02

Assess the potential

The assessment looks at both patentability and commercial potential. It considers questions such as novelty, technical feasibility, market relevance, and the route into application.

03

Prepare the patent application

If the assessment is positive, a patent application is prepared and filed with the relevant patent office. BayPAT then supports the next stage of the patenting process.

04

Find the right route into application

The next step is to identify the right route into industry. BayPAT draws on its network to identify suitable partners and move technologies toward licensing and practical application.

05

Explore the start-up route

Some inventions are better developed through a new venture. In those cases, the path can extend into start-up questions, licensing structure, and the move from invention to company formation.

06

Negotiate the right agreement

Once interested partners or spin-outs have been identified, the next step is negotiation and contract design. Agreements are tailored to the invention and the partner context.
