For Founders and Start-ups

Finding the right route for your start-up

Founders and start-ups enter the transfer process from different starting points. Some already have IP and need clarity on protection, patenting, and market potential. Others are looking for technologies that could become the basis of a new venture.

I have IPI am looking for technology

For founders with IP

If you already have an invention or technology, the key question is often what to do next. BayPAT can support the route from IP-related questions into patenting, market analysis, and commercialization.

IP strategy

Patents can shape a start-up's competitive position. BayPAT can help clarify how protection strategy fits the company’s development and where IP should play the strongest role.

Discuss IP strategy

Patentability assessment

Before committing to the next step, founders often need a clearer view of whether patenting is worth pursuing. BayPAT can assess novelty, inventive step, applicability, and the broader strength of the IP position.

Check patentability

Market analysis

IP alone is not enough. Market analysis helps clarify where an invention creates value, how it compares with alternatives, and whether it supports the case for further investment.

Discuss market analysis

For founders seeking IP

Not every start-up begins with its own invention. Some teams are looking for a strong technology base with commercial potential. For them, the path starts with finding the right opportunity and understanding how it could fit the business.

Technology search

BayPAT’s technology landscape brings together inventions from Bavarian universities and research institutes. This gives founders a route into technologies that may be suitable for licensing, development, or company formation.

Explore technology offers

Fit and potential

The key question is not only what a technology does, but whether it fits the intended market, team, and start-up model. BayPAT can help shape that early discussion.
