About BayPAT

At the interface between science and industry

BayPAT is a university-owned company that supports the protection and commercialization of intellectual property from Bavarian research. It connects strong ideas with the structures, partners, and transfer routes needed to move them into application.

Our mission and expertise

BayPAT was founded in 2007 by the Bavarian universities and universities of applied sciences to strengthen technology transfer in Bavaria. Since then, its core task has remained the same: evaluate inventions, shape protection strategies, and move promising technologies toward commercialization.

Why this role matters

Technology transfer depends on more than a patent filing or a single commercial contact. It requires evaluation, strategic judgement, market understanding, and the ability to connect academic research with the right external partners. BayPAT brings these elements together in one structure.

Expertise in practice

BayPAT combines experience in patenting, licensing, early-stage invention support, and commercialization. That expertise is applied across different audiences, from universities and inventors to industry and start-ups.

What BayPAT does

BayPAT’s work spans the route from IP-related decisions to patenting and commercialization. The services page explains these areas in more detail, but the core role can be understood through four recurring themes.

IP strategy

BayPAT supports decisions around protection strategy, portfolio thinking, and the role IP should play in the route from research to application.

Patentability and prior-art review

Before an invention moves forward, BayPAT helps assess whether protection is realistic and worthwhile.

Market analysis

BayPAT looks not only at technology, but also at where it could create value, what market context matters, and how a path into application may look.

IP commercialization

Commercialization includes the strategy, partner route, and contractual framework needed to turn protected technology into a usable opportunity.

Connected across research, industry, and transfer

BayPAT’s network spans Bavarian universities and research institutes, industry partners, investors, and the wider transfer community. That breadth matters because commercialization depends on making the right connections at the right time.

Research

BayPAT works with 28 Bavarian universities, universities of applied sciences, and research institutes, and also collaborates internationally where IP and transfer routes require it.

Industry

Through its long-developed network in life sciences and physical sciences, BayPAT connects technologies with relevant companies, licensees, and investors.

Technology transfer ecosystem

BayPAT also works within regional, national, and international transfer structures. Associations and transfer networks help shape the broader conditions under which knowledge and technology transfer can succeed.
